ΠΩΛΟΎΝΤΑΙ ΕΙΣ ΤΑΣ ΤΡΑΠΕΖΑΣ

TREASURY BILLS
SOLD AT ALL THE BANKS

Speaker
Greece’s government, mainly the military sector.
Occasion
Greece had just entered WWI, mainly with intention to take Istanbul from the Ottomans.
Audience
Greek citizens, primarily those who have money and have not purchased t-bills.
Purpose
To encourage Greek citizens to purchase t-bills.
Subject
T-bills.
Strategies
Symbolism
The two-headed eagle is a symbol of power and majesty, and has been used by many countries to represent such. In this case, the eagle is representative of Greece.
The Ottoman empire is symbolized by their flag. It is being grabbed by the eagle, representing how Greece wants to seize Istanbul and several other cities from the Ottomans.
Exaggeration
The eagle in the foreground is larger than Istanbul in the back. This implies the eagle is massive, and will likely take Istanbul easily.
Generalization/Ad hominem
Representing the Ottomans as a non-living object dehumanizes the Ottomans. It also subtly implies that the Ottomans will be defeated easily.
Appeal to religion/authority
The double headed eagle is a well known symbol of the Orthodox Church, including the Greek Orthodox Church. This shows some of the Greek’s motives for wanting Istanbul, as Istanbul has been historically fought over by Christians and Muslims.
