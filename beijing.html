PHẢI!
CHÍNH BẠN
HAY CHỐNG BỌN
TAY SAI CỦA BẮC-KINH

ENDURE!
MAKE A HABIT OF BEING AGAINST
THE HENCHMEN OF THE TERRIFYING NORTH [Beijing, China]

Speaker
South Vietnam (cannot find exact source). Specifically, those in South Vietnam that are against China.
Occasion
In the 1950s (Vietnam War era), Vietnam and China had a very poor relationship. As well, Vietnam was about to split into North and South Vietnam. Communist China was aiding North Vietnam with military pursuits.
Audience
Those in South Vietnam, especially those who are neutral or on the fence in opposing China.
Purpose
To unite Vietnamese people against the Chinese, or push them to a decision.
Subject
Portraying China in a negative light.
Strategies
Inclusive language
Bright colors bring attention to the reader’s involvement in resistance to China (“chính bạn” means “yourself”)
The man pointing to the reader provides even more reader involvement.
Allusion
While perhaps not intentional, the man on the poster very much resembles the famous WWI poster, “What Are You Doing for Preparedness?” (“I Want You”). The man is also very Westernized in art style.
Loaded terms
“Bắc-kinh” meaning “terrifying north”, as well as “tay sai” [henchmen], demonizes China, despite what China has or hasn’t done.
