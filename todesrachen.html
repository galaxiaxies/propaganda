HEUTE SIND SIE DARAN–MORGEN KOMME ICH!
Der Todesrachen.

TODAY IT IS YOUR TURN–TOMORROW I WILL GO!
The jaws of death.

Daimler aktien
Kriegsgewinn

Daimler (a German company) shares
War profits


Speaker
Anti-war organization/group.
Occasion
Germany was fighting in WWI
Audience
People who have not enlisted in WWI
Purpose
To discourage the reader from enlisting in the army
Subject
The death seen from fighting/war.
Strategies
Appeal to fear
The soldiers are jumping into the skeleton’s mouth and coming out dead. This is meant to discourage people from joining the war.
The man in white is holding a whip to whip the soldiers into the skeleton. This implies that those jumping into the skeleton’s mouth are being forced into doing such.
Post Hoc
The soldiers dying after jumping into the skeleton’s mouth directly implies that going to war results only in death.
Ad Hominem
While I cannot find evidence that the two large men are specific people, they are clearly caricatures of at least the aristocratic enlisting people into the war. The man in black is holding pieces of paper saying “war profits” and company shares.
Appeal to fear
The text says that today it is the reader’s turn (to die) and tomorrow will be the author’s.
Loaded terms
“Todesrachen” can be translated as “the jaws of death”. Alternatively, it can be translated as “the dragon of death”. These both invoke negative feelings in the reader.
